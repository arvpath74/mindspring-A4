Problem Solving

Problem solving is a critical capability in complex digital environments. It involves analysing issues, identifying practical solutions, and implementing approaches that balance technical constraints, risk, and organisational priorities. At MindSpring Collective, this is particularly important in delivering secure, scalable, and user-centred digital services.

I have demonstrated strong problem-solving capability across a range of technical and operational challenges, developing solutions that are both innovative and practical.

Project Examples

Phased Technology Platform Migration

I developed a solution to enable the successful migration of a major technology platform without that derisks the approach away from a 'big bang' approach, and enabled a phased implementation approach. The challenge was to transition from an existing system whilt maintaining service continuity and minimising disruption to MindSpring's participants.

The solution established a dual-database architecture that could be accessed through a single front-end experience. This approach allowed both systems to operate in parallel, and features to be deployed from the legacy platform to the new platform progressively.

By implementing a phased migration strategy, the organisation was able to reduce risk, maintain service stability, and continuously validate the new platform before the full transition was complete.

Technical Security Incident Response and Remediation

I led the design of a solution following a major operational and technical security breach within an online communication platform. The incident highlighted limitations in user identification and monitoring capabilities, creating risks to platform integrity and organisational standards.

The solution involved implementing multi-factor authentication (MFA) to strengthen user identity verification and improve visibility of user activity. This ensured that access to the platform was more securely controlled and aligned with organisational security requirements.

This approach significantly improved the organisation’s ability to monitor activity, enforce appropriate usage, and reduce the likelihood of future security incidents.
